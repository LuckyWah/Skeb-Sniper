Gmail Data We Access

The application requires access to Gmail user data through the Gmail API. We use the Gmail Read-Only scope to access the following email information:

  • History ID: Prevents processing old emails by ensuring only emails received after the app starts are accessed.
  • Recipient: Filters out emails intended for others by verifying that the recipient matches your email address, as multiple users may share the same subscription.
  • Sender: Filters emails based on the sender by allowing only those from our predefined list.
  • Body: Retrieves the body of filtered emails for interpretation.

We do not use any other Google API scopes. Therefore, we do not modify, delete, or send emails as it is not permitted in the Read-Only scope.

How We Use Your Data

The application only accesses and processes your Gmail data in real-time for the following purposes:

  • Detecting new emails by monitoring Gmail metadata via Pub/Sub notifications.
  • Retrieving new emails upon receiving a Pub/Sub notification.
  • Filtering out irrelevant emails by checking if the sender is in the predefined list. If not, the email is ignored.
  • Extracting the subject and body only from relevant emails.
  • Analyzing the email subject and body to interpret its content and categorize it based on the content.

The application does not write, store, or transfer your data as the emails are processed in real-time and discarded after the above process is complete.

Data Retention and Deletion

The application does not retain any data, including information about your Gmail account or email contents. All Gmail data is processed in real-time and is never stored or transferred. Therefore, data deletion is not applicable.

If you wish to stop using the application, you can revoke access at any time via your Google Account settings. Revoking access will immediately prevent our application from accessing your Gmail data.

Gmail Token Protection and Security

We prioritize the security of your Gmail account by implementing strict protective measures:

  • All API requests comply with Google's OAuth 2.0 authentication protocol.
  • We use multi-layer encryption to encrypt your Gmail OAuth token stored on your machine.
  • We do not store or transfer your Gmail token.
